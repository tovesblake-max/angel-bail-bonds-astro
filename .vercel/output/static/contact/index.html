Frequently Asked Questions

What is a bail bond? ▼

A bail bond is a contract where we (Angel Bail Bonds) agree to pay the full bail amount to the court if you fail to appear at your court date. You pay us a non-refundable premium (typically 10% of the bail amount) instead of paying the full bail to the court.

How quickly can you get someone out of jail? ▼

In most cases, we can have someone released within 1-2 hours of posting the bail bond. The exact time depends on the jail's processing time. Call us immediately when bail is set and we'll work as fast as possible.

Do you offer payment plans? ▼

Yes! We understand that bail can be a financial burden. We work with most clients to create flexible payment plans that fit their budget. Call us to discuss your options.

What do I need to get a bail bond? ▼

You'll need: (1) The inmate's name and booking number, (2) The jail location, (3) The bail amount set by the judge, (4) Proof of income or ability to pay the premium, and (5) A valid photo ID.

What happens if they miss court? ▼

If the defendant fails to appear, you (or the co-signer) become liable for the full bail amount. A warrant will be issued and we may contact you to help locate them. This is why choosing a responsible co-signer is critical.

Are you available 24/7? ▼

Yes! We're available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Arrests don't happen on a schedule, and neither do we take time off. Call (405) 614-3000 anytime.
